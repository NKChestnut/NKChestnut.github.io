About Me

I am a Data Science student with a background in machine learning, statistical analysis, and data visualization. My work/research interest lies in applying data science/math principals in applied/computational industries (engineering, finance, etc). I thrive on solving challenging problems and am always eager to learn new technologies and methodologies.

With experience in Python (Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn), R, and various data visualization tools, I am experienced at the entire data science lifecycle, from data collection and cleaning to model deployment and interpretation.

My Presentations

Presentations

Mapping Engagement: Analyzing Visitor Trends and Regional Impact for TCMU

Examines membership trends and visitation patterns at The Children’s Museum of the Upstate (TCMU) through a full statistical analysis of geographic origins, membership/donation records, and visitation trends. Studies how local and outside visitors contribute to the broader economic impact of TCMU, which supports strategic choices focused on intensifying tourism initiatives and community outreach.

"Localize to Globalize: The Consequences of Data Localization to Combat Digital Exploitation"

THE PENDULUM Clemson's Premier International Affairs Publication, Vol. 20, pp.26-29 (2024)

I critically examine the rise of data localization laws as a response to digital colonialism. The article explores how these policies, while aimed at combating digital exploitation, can lead to significant unintended economic and technological consequences.
