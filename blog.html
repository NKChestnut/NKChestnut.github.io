Reflections on Digital Scrapbooking

Published on June 28, 2025

The concept of a "digital scrapbook" has always fascinated me. It's a space where I can document things I think about—music I like recently, countries I love, ideas I admire... but I ask myself why? Am I not just yapping about something I like, but rather why I think it's important to curate these digital memories?

This is the full content of "Reflections on Digital Scrapbooking".

Digital scrapbooking offers a unique way to preserve thoughts, inspirations, and daily life without the physical clutter. It allows for easy organization, sharing, and integration of various media types, from text to images and even embedded videos. It's a personal archive that grows with you.

It's about creating a personal space on the web that feels like a cozy corner, a digital garden where ideas can blossom and be revisited. This approach contrasts with the ephemeral nature of social media, offering a more permanent and curated reflection of one's journey.

My Journey into Web Design

Published on June 20, 2025

I've been interested in design ever since I started messing around with Google Slides' templates. During the start of 2021, with people hating corporate/flat design, I decided to start designing in a "skeuomorphic" style. This blog post details my learning process and inspirations.

This is the full content of "My Journey into Web Design".

My design journey began with simple tools like Google Slides, but I quickly moved to more robust platforms like Figma. The shift towards "skeuomorphic" design was a conscious choice, as I appreciate the tactile and familiar feel it brings to digital interfaces, contrasting with the often sterile flat design trends.

Learning Inkscape and Blender has opened up new avenues for creating custom graphics and 3D elements, further enhancing the ability to craft unique and immersive web experiences. The goal is to build websites that are not just functional but also visually rich and engaging, reflecting a blend of old-web charm with modern capabilities.

My Latest Plant Obsession: Terrariums

Published on June 18, 2025

There's something incredibly satisfying about creating a miniature ecosystem in a glass container. This post shares my recent dive into the world of terrariums, from choosing the right plants to maintaining a healthy environment.

Full content for "My Latest Plant Obsession: Terrariums".

Terrariums are self-sustaining miniature gardens that require minimal maintenance once established. They're perfect for small spaces and bring a touch of nature indoors. I've been experimenting with various mosses, ferns, and small, humidity-loving plants.

The process involves layering drainage material, charcoal, substrate, and then carefully arranging the plants. Sealing the terrarium creates a humid microclimate where plants thrive. It's a peaceful and rewarding hobby!
